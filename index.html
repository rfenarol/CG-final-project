Charlotte Perriand

Paris, 1903-1999

Charlotte Perriand is a full part of the cultural avant-garde of the early decades of the twentieth century. She promoted a profound renewal of aesthetic values, and gave birth to a properly modern sensibility of everyday life. In this context, its specific contribution focuses on the spaces of the interior, which are conceived as an engine of a new way of living, which is still at the center of the contemporary lifestyle.

In the field of the history of the twentieth century, the advent of modernity is made possible by the enterprising boldness of this real reform of the interior. When she was only twenty-four, began a ten-year collaboration with Le Corbusier and Pierre Jeanneret, at the famous atelier of 35, rue de SÃ¨vres in Paris. Her presence in the atelier of Le Corbusier is visible in all the furniture designed. Charlotte Perriand becomes a cornerstone of the renovation project promoted by the architect, notably by adding a dimension of humanity to the sometimes cold rationalism Le Corbusier.

Models of furniture design

In her projects she manages to animate the basic objects of everyday life with new aesthetic values: in particular, his talent and intuition in the discovery and use of new materials are to appear in their full extent. The feature which characterizes the personality of Charlotte Perriand is an honest commitment to the principles of a human rationalism and innovator, who has preserved in her works, which have been occupied with passion.

Ventaglio

Table in natural oak or stained black. The plan is composed of fourteen staves, the base has three legs. The particular configuration of the floor and the arrangement of the legs allows a dynamic and flexible use of the table, also by a number of persons greater than a traditional configuration.

The model of the table has two parts:

  • Surface
  • Legs

The surface has been created assembling a central part with two other lateral parts, rotated by a suitable angle. Each of them is an easy composition of cuboids and simplicial complex. The effect of the division into 14 staves is obtained by drawing coloured lines all over the surface. Below there is a prototype of the surface developed using the python module Pyplasm:

Ombra

Armchair with frame in chromed steel or painted black. Pillows are removable, the padding is in polyurethane and polyester wadding.

The model has been decomposed in 3 parts:

  • Legs
  • Support
  • Pillows

Modelling legs has been the hardest part of the work, because of their angular shape. They've been obtained mixing more than one Bezier surface. Legs and support together form the frame of this armchair. Below there is a prototype of the frame developed using the python module Pyplasm:

Petalo

From the passion for the color of Charlotte Perriand comes this system of five small tables. The plans, triangular in shape with rounded corners, are lacquered in the colors: red, gray, yellow, green and blue. The sinuous structure, which seems designed with a single stroke of pen, is in matt black painted metal.

The model of every small table has two parts:

  • Plan
  • Legs

This is a first prototype of a single table developed using the python module Pyplasm:

The plan has been modeled as a combination of Bezier surfaces. A difficult part of the work has been to represent legs and junctions between them. As is visible in the prototype, they've been designed starting from simple Bezier curves. So, these has been used to create a cubic hermite surface in order to reach a cylindrical frame turning around the plan and composing legs. Below there is the final model developed using Plasm.js:

To obtain the system of five small tables, I've used this easy but powerful "trick", available thanks to the functional paradigm of the Plasm languages:

var rotationLeft = R([X,Z])(PI/4);
var scale = S([X,Y,Z])([0.8,0.8,0.8]);
var translDown = T([X,Y])([0.2,-0.06]);
var tranf = COMP([scale,rotationLeft,translDown]);
var model = STRUCT(REPLICA(5)([model,tranf]));

Tokyo Outdoor

Chaise longue, with backrest consisting of 12 strips curved in teak, beech or bamboo and a curved cradle made by the same essence. Basament is in solid teak or beech or solid bamboo rebuilt.

The model is structured in two main parts:

  • Frame
  • Basement

The frame is in turn divided into two sub-components:

  • Cradle
  • Backrest

A prototype of the frame, developed using the python module Pyplasm is visible below:

The 12 strips of the backrest is the most interesting part of the model, created starting from a single curved stripe and replicated applying a small rotation and translation to each copy with a solution similar to the one seen previously:

var rotationLeft = R([X,Z])(PI/180);
var traslationLeft = T([Z])([0.45]);
var tranfLeft = COMP([rotationLeft,traslationLeft]);
var backrestLeft = STRUCT(REPLICA(6)([backrest,tranfLeft]));
var rotationRight = R([X,Z])(-PI/180);
var traslationRight = T([Z])([-0.45]);
var tranfRight = COMP([rotationRight,traslationRight]);
var backrestRight = STRUCT(REPLICA(6)([backrest,tranfRight]));

Each stripe is a combination of Bezier surfaces and semicircular parts. The basement is composed of 4 cylinders representing the legs, joined by some conjunctive parts to give support to the cradle. This is the final model developed using Plasm.js:
